Security

Trading on an online cryptocurrency exchange requires you to make an online account and because these exchanges are made by technology enthusiests, your account is often equiped with the most secure standards in online security. Two factor authentication, a means of double checking with you that you are you, works by contacting your cellphone, email or connecting with another online account.

Risks

Eggs in one basket.

As with any centralized system, cryptocurrency exchanges have all the assets are stored in one place. If a hacker is able to penetrate the systems defenses, they could gain access to all the crypto people store on the exchange and this has happened in the past. Notably MtGox, an exchange that no longer exists, was hacked in 2014. Millions of dollars of bitcoin was lost and not returned to the public.

However, some modern exchanges such as asdfasdf provide insurance for their users. If they get hacked they will replace your stolen crypto. You also have the option of storing your crypto in your own wallet but possibly the best option for an exchange is for it to be decentralized by the same blockchain technology it is servicing
